Pharmacists are just as essential as any other health care provider for treating patients' conditions and advising them on their health concerns. You will become an expert in the science of medicine and with that you ensure patients receive the correct dosage of life-saving prescriptions, advise the other health care professionals on pharmacology, and be able to identify when one drug is dangerous or interacts with another. You are also a key component in offering tips for health, well-being, stress management, nutrition, how to stop smoking, and other lifestyle changes that will save people’s lives.

Foreign pharmacist:

  1. Get your diploma evaluation report from ECE. Click Here.
  2. Pass the FPEGEE examination Click Here.
  3. Pass the TOEFL with the required score by the NABP (National Board of Pharmacy).
  4. Get an internship and complete 1,500 hours in a community pharmacy and at least 8 hours in a hospital pharmacy.
  5. Pass the NAPLEX exam.
  6. Pass the CPJE ( if practicing in the state of CA ) or MPJE ( if any other state except CA) And congratulation you’re officially a US pharmacist.

    7. - Duration of the process: 6 years in Algeria + 3 years at least in the USA.

    - Average salary: $60 - $90

Through a US school:

  1. Earn a bachelor’s degree in a subject like biology, chemistry or biochemistry. If you pursue a non-science major you can still take prerequisite courses like chemistry, organic chemistry, anatomy and physiology.
  2. Choose a school of pharmacy that corresponds to your preferences in terms of cost/completion time and location.
  3. The school of choice needs to be had accreditation from the Accreditation Council for Pharmacy Education (ACPE).
  4. Send your application to many schools to increase the chance of being accepted into a pharmD program.
  5. After earning the PharmD degree you will have to pass the two examination NAPLEX and CPJE (only for CA state) or MPJE (for the other states).
    • Duration of the process: 3 years bachelor’s degree in science + 3/4 years in a US pharmacy School.
    • To work in a retail pharmacy these steps mentionned above are enough (Average salary: $60 - $90).
    • To become a Clinical pharmacist, Oncology pharmacist, Nuclear pharmacist or Ambulatory care pharmacist you need to get into a residency program with one to two years more studying (Avg salary $80 - $110)
